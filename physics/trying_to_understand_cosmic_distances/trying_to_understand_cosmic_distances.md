# Trying to understand cosmic distances

**Author:** Antoine Brunet

**Created:** April 8, 2026

Our nearest star, Proxima Centauri, is about 4.24 light years away or about 40 trillion km away. If one were to travel from Earth to Proxima Centauri by car at 100 km/h without stopping, it would take one about 45 million years. Let's take something faster than a car. During the Apollo 11 mission, it took the crew 3 days to get to the Moon. The Moon has an average distance of about 400,000 km from Earth. Proxima Centauri is about a million times farther. I am starting to feel the distance as I am writing and that's our closest star.

Now, let us leave our home galaxy, the Milky Way. Our nearest major galaxy, Andromeda, is about 2.5 million light years away from Earth. That is about more than 1 followed by 19 zeros km. Let us talk about 1 followed by 19 zeros. It is 10 quintillions. It is one million trillion. It is an understatement to state that Andromeda is a lot of km from Earth. Andromeda is about more than half a million times farther from Earth than Proxima Centauri is. That is far!

We are not done yet. Let us now talk about the observable universe. It has a radius of about 46.5 billion light years. It is about more than 1 followed by 23 zeros km. Let us talk about 1 followed by 23 zeros. It is 100 sextillions. It is one billion trillion. The radius of the observable universe is about more than 10,000 times larger than the distance between Earth and Andromeda!
